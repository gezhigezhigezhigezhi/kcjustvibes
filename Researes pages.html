About Us

We are a non-profit website dedicated to helping and providing aids to at-risk women in Austin, Texas. In our city alone, we have over 2,000 people experiencing homelessness with at least 40% of these people being women. Around the country,there is a rise in homelessness due to fast population growths in major cities, which in turn contributes to rising housing costs. Almost every group of people are affected by homelessness including veterans, survivors of domestic violence, young children, runaway youth, refugees, and immigrant families. Our aim is to provide resources to empower women with information from our database to help them meet their needs in various areas.

Mential Illness/Addiction

According to the Mayo Clinic, a mental illness is defined to be any disorder that causes a change in a person’s behavior or mood, and disrupts a person’s ability to function [2]. Mental illness is thought to affect 25% of the homeless population in America, even being the third largest cause for homelessness. It can hinder a person’s ability to create and maintain stable relationships, jobs, and affect an individual's ability to perform daily care activities [3]. The National Bureau of Economic Research reports that there is a high correlation between individuals suffering from a mental illness and self-medication. Among individuals suffering from a diagnosed mental illness, about 69% suffer from alcoholism, 84% suffer from cocaine addiction, and 68% suffer from nicotine addiction [4].

Domestic Violence

According to the Family and Youth Services Bureau, more than 80% of homeless women and children report having experienced domestic violence. Almost 57% of homeless women report that domestic violence was the number one cause for their homelessness. Furthermore, about 38% of all domestic violence victims will become homeless at some point in their lifetime [5].

Unemplayment/Underaployment

Unemployment is defined as individuals who are currently unemployed but are actively seeking employment. Underemployment is defined as individuals who are highly skilled but are not working at their full capacity [6]. Although unemployment rates are declining, homelessness rates continue to increase due to the aftermath of the Great Recession as well as wages not keeping up with the rising costs of living [7]. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the current unemployment rate in Austin is 2.6%.

Healthcare and Family Planning

In general, 73% of homeless women are unable to meet medical, dental, mental, prenatal, and other preventative care. They face higher rates of chronic illnesses including asthma, mental diseases, anemia, bronchitis, and hypertension among other problems. Furthermore, homeless women have higher pregnancy and unintended pregnancy rates than the rest of the population. Homeless women are more often provided with user-based failure rates rather than long-acting reversible contraceptives. They are more likely to have preterm deliveries than other women [8].

Single Parenthood and Family Services

Family homelessness is a growing issue regardless of race, and is due to the lack of affordable housing, and increasing costs of living. It is estimated that about 37% of all homeless populations are families [9]. The Austin Area Command of the the Salvation Army reports that approximately 100 mothers and over 200 children are on the waiting list every day for emergency shelter [10].

Public Health Data for Research Purposes

The purposes of this anonymous survey is to collect data from the users of this website in order to better understand the needs of the at-risk homeless populations of Austin. With this information, we hope to develop models that could better establish cause and effect relationships between different factors in order to eventually construct better policies geared towards helping the at-risk populations in Austin.
